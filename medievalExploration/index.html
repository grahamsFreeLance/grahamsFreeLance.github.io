Son of Lord Escapes from Frankish Prison

This morning in a development from Frankish territory the Indian Ocean Today received news that the son of the Lord of Mount Sinai, missing for a year and presumed dead after he left for a hunting trip and did not return, has resurfaced after a daring escape. Salim Bishara, 19, left for a hunting trip over 18 months ago and was never found after extensive searching. During the hunt he was set upon by a contingent of Franks who detained him and transported him back to the castle at Bayt Jibril. The lord of Bayt Jibril set ransom for his captive at the sum of 2,000 dinars; however, news of this ransom never reached back to Bishara's father. Lord Giroud of Bayt Jibril was unavailabe for comment as to the nature of the ransom request or his failure to ensure the news of the ransom reached Bishara's father. Bishara credits the success of his daring escape both to his own resourcefulnes and the aid of a bedouin, name currently unknown. More information concerning this story will be printed as it is investigated
